State actions against drought

September 23, 2021

Governor Gavin Newsom signed a package of climate action bills that include $5.2 billion for drought response and long-term water resilience. The measures will secure and expand water supplies, support water infrastructure, improve water security and quality, and support wildlife.

July 28, 2021

Governor Newsom signs AB 148, which allows state agencies to expedite funding for areas experiencing drought.

July 8, 2021

Governor Newsom calls on Californians to reduce water use by 15% to help protect water reserves if drought conditions continue. Drought emergency proclamations expanded to include Inyo, Marin, Mono, Monterey, San Luis Obispo, San Mateo, Santa Barbara, Santa Clara and Santa Cruz counties.
