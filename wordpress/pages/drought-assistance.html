If you need bottled or hauled water, your county’s Office of Emergency Services (OES) can help. The state funds county response to drought-related water shortages. 

Emergency water is available to households, small water systems, and community water systems. 

Contact your county’s OES if you have a water outage, shortage, or dry well. They will determine what help is available and how to get it to you.

The drought is an evolving emergency. The State will adapt its response as needs change.  

More help for households

Some counties are struggling to help their residents deal with the impact of drought. If your county OES is unable to assist you, email drought@waterboards.ca.gov.

Steps for community water systems

Community water systems serve more than 15 service connections or 25 residents. Getting emergency water to a community water system requires a few extra steps. Here’s how to get help:

  1. Follow the steps in your system’s water shortage contingency plan.
  2. Contact your county’s OES to request help. They will determine what help is available and how to get it to you.
  3. Alert your district engineer or local primary agency (LPA) to the issue.
    1. If the State Water Board regulates your system, contact your district engineer.
    2. If your county regulates your system, contact your LPA.

Small community grants 

The Small Community Drought Relief Program helps communities that are not served by an urban water supplier. This is defined as one that supplies at least 3,000 connections or 3,000 acre-feet of water per year. 

To get a grant, a project must serve communities like this in counties under drought emergency. Small communities affected by drought are encouraged to apply as soon as possible. Submit applications or questions to SmallCommunityDrought@water.ca.gov.
